Geodesic Rhombic Triacontahedra

For these pages, a "geodesic rhombic triacontahedron" is a polyhedron derived from a Rhombic Triacontahedron by subdividing each face into smaller faces using an appropriately shear-mapped square grid, and then applying a canonicalization algorithm [1] to make the result more spherical. The procedure is analogous to the way a geodesic cube is derived from a cube.
